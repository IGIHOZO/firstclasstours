Sure, here are some facts about gorilla trekking in Rwanda:

Rwanda is home to about a third of the world's remaining mountain gorillas, making it a popular destination for gorilla trekking.

The gorilla trekking experience in Rwanda takes place in Volcanoes National Park, located in the northwestern part of the country.

Permits are required to go gorilla trekking in Rwanda, and they cost $1,500 per person for a one-hour encounter with the gorillas.

Only eight people are allowed to visit each gorilla family group per day, to minimize disturbance to the gorillas and their habitat.

Visitors are required to be at least 15 years old to go gorilla trekking in Rwanda.

Gorilla trekking is a physically demanding activity and requires a good level of fitness. The altitude in the park can reach up to 3,000 meters (9,800 feet) above sea level.

Trekkers are accompanied by guides and porters who help to carry bags and provide assistance during the hike.

While sightings of the gorillas are not guaranteed, visitors have a high chance of seeing them. The experience of being in close proximity to these majestic creatures is a once-in-a-lifetime opportunity.

Gorilla trekking in Rwanda is a highly regulated activity to protect the gorillas and their habitat. Visitors are required to follow strict guidelines to minimize their impact on the gorillas and the environment
